Coronavirus

Our Goal

By now, I am sure everyone has come across a website, post, flyer, whatever it may be, telling you how to take precautions that help prevent individuals contracting COVID-19 or spreading it to others. One of the best ways is through social distancing, but there are a lot of added consequences (or benefits) that come along with this. We hope this page to be a place that have compiled a list of resources on tips ranging from staying busy during quarantine, keeping your mental healthy happy, maintaining relationships, and of course, stopping the spread.

In the future, we see this website as a place for users to share their experiences, ideas, and activities that have been done during this time as a way for others to learn, or simply just for a fun read!

When Going Out...

It is important to take every precaution when going outside during this pandemic, especially with cases rising. This also means staying home unless you are going outside for something completely necessary, such as groceries or because you are an essential worker. Below are resources that will educate and help you continue to social distance safely.

1. Learn how to make your own custom face masks!

• Use the official guide on the CDC website to create simple masks in no time. (Some require no sewing!)

• Use this video on Youtube to help you create a mask of your own (no sewing required)!

** If you have the time and ability, donate masks at your local hospital or where ever healthcare workers are dealing with COVID-19 patients! Before dropping of the masks, be sure to call and set up a time that works for them. They are fighting on the frontlines, and the least we can do to thank them is provide them with some extra protection. **

- Helpful resource: The UC Berekely School of Public Health has compiled a list hospital from across the country that is in need of masks on this document.

Staying Home

While staying home can seem like a bore, with the right mindset, it is possible to make quarantine a productive time in your life, from exploring new Hobbis to participating in programs such as Kode with Klossy! Here are a few ways to make this massive amount of free time into something a little more useful. (But don't overwork yourself. Reward yourself with breaks, you deserve it.)

1. Google generic lists of activities to do during quarantine, or something a little more catered to your own taste.

2. Use the Hobbi website to discover new hobbies and look into upcoming local events.

3. Volunteer from home by connecting with organizations such as the UN, Crisis Hotlines and more!

4. Use these websites to learn a new instrument, or sharpen skills on one you already know how to play!

5. Look into ways to make $$$ during quarantine. If you're a high school or college student, perhaps a tutoring job may be right up your alley. If you are qualified in a certain subject, look into ways online to make those skills useful in a time of isolation. If you're of age and feel comfortable to do so, get a job at the local market, though this is not reccomended due to the higher risk of contracting COVID-19. Mental Health Resources

Staying healthy in a time of isolation, or in a time of stress with managing family life, relationships, and work all at home.

• National Alliance on Mental Illness : Online community that allows people to share their thoughts and postivity!

• 18percent : Allows users to connect and talk to peers about their mental health.

• ForLikeMinds : Specialized for quarantine, also allows user to connect with peers to talk about mental health and worrying moments in life.

• Betterhelp : Pairs users with a licensed counselor. Sessions can be attended individually or in groups, as needed.
